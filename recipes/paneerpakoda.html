Paneer Pakoda

Description

Paneer pakoda is a popular Indian snack made by deep-frying slices of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) coated in a flavorful chickpea flour batter. Crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, paneer pakodas are enjoyed as a tasty appetizer or tea-time snack.

Ingridients

  • Paneer (Indian cottage cheese), sliced
  • Chickpea flour (besan)
  • Rice flour (optional, for extra crispiness)
  • Carom seeds (ajwain)
  • Turmeric powder
  • Red chili powder
  • Coriander powder
  • Garam masala
  • Salt
  • Water
  • Oil, for deep frying

Steps:

  1. In a bowl, prepare the batter by mixing chickpea flour, rice flour (if using), carom seeds, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Gradually add water to form a smooth and thick batter.
  2. Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadhai over medium heat.
  3. Dip each slice of paneer into the prepared batter, ensuring it's coated evenly.
  4. Carefully drop the coated paneer slices into the hot oil and fry until they turn golden brown and crispy on all sides.
  5. Once done, remove the paneer pakodas from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels.
  6. Serve hot with your favorite chutney or sauce. Enjoy the delicious crispy paneer pakodas!
